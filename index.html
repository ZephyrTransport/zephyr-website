About

Mission

The Foundation’s mission is to advance rigorous transportation and land use decision-making for the public good by advocating for, facilitating, and supporting improved travel analysis and forecasting methods.

Vision

Be the primary venue through which leading researchers and practitioners seek to develop and improve travel analysis methods and tools that are demonstrably valuable, credible, transparent, tractable, reproducible, and usable.

Goals

The Foundation’s goals are to support, incubate, guide, encourage, teach, and convene, as follows:

Support

Provide robust infrastructure, including financial, administrative, and legal support.

Incubate

Incubate innovative and useful planning methods in preparation for deployment by regions, cities, and neighborhoods.

Guide

Guide research towards useful avenues and products.

Encourage

Encourage the use of open data standards, rigorous development practices, interoperability, reproducible research methods, and evaluation of research.

Teach

Teach core competencies and next generation travel analysis skills to researchers and practitioners.

Convene

Convene leading academics and practitioners to guide the Foundation’s efforts.

Principles

The Foundation is guided by the following principles:

Respectful, honest, technical-based interaction;
Collegial and collaborative;
Driven by useful innovation;
Acting in support of the public good;
User-friendly packaging and licensing;
Consistently high-quality work products;
Faithful implementation of standards; and,
Lawfulness as mandatory.

Why a Foundation?

We have been asked [ and have asked ourselves ] why we think a new Foundation is the best structure for improving the travel analysis industry. The short answer is that our current options for furthering our profession have various drawbacks that prevent them from pushing our industry forward. Specific attributes that we need include: flexibility, agility, stability [ of staffing and funding ], and ability to contribute funding from public and private sources on multiple levels.

Finer Print

Governance

The Foundation will be directed by a five-member volunteer board of diverse backgrounds. The founding stakeholders [ a pool of individuals selected to represent the will of the industry and those that it serves ] will select the initial board. The board will guide the organization, selecting officers to oversee the day-to-day affairs of the Foundation and forming committees to focus on the immediate tasks at hand. We believe the Apache Foundation serves as an excellent organizational model.

The initial board members must embrace the significant task of getting the organization off the ground. This will include seeking contributors, handling a host of administrative duties, and continually making the case for the Foundation’s existence. For us to succeed, the board’s duties in year one will be very different than their duties in year five.

In the future, the bulk of the day-to-day work of the Foundation will be completed within the structure of project management committees. The board will create project management committees and select's their chair but does not interfere with the technical decision-making of the committee.

Business Plan [ in brief ]

We estimate annual costs during our first five years to be approximately $400,000. Costs include legal assistance, insurance, accounting services, board member travel, and other administrative overhead, as well as salaries and office space for two (2) full-time staff equivalents. During the start-up phase, consultants will staff the organization and will be directly overseen by board-appointed officers. Our revenue plan seeks annual contributions in the $5,000 to $25,000 range from a variety of governmental entities, including:

  • Metropolitan Planning Organizations;
  • State Departments of Transportation;
  • Federal Highway and Transit Administrations;
  • University Transportation Centers;
  • Associations of Governments, e.g., National Association of City Transportation Officials (NACTO), Association of Metropolitan Planning Organizations (AMPO), American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials (AASHTO).

At an annual contribution level of $10,000, approximately 40 contributing entities are required to sustain our expected baseline operations.
